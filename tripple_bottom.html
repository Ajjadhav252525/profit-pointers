Triple Bottom


The triple bottom pattern is a well-known and bullish reversal formation in technical analysis, indicating a potential shift from a downtrend to an uptrend. This pattern is characterized by three distinct price lows that appear to form a pattern resembling the letter "W" on a price chart.
The first price low represents the culmination of the preceding downtrend, followed by a rally that fails to surpass the previous highs. The price subsequently declines to form the second low, often at a similar level to the first low, creating the first trough of the triple bottom. Following this, a moderate rally takes place but again encounters resistance and results in the formation of the third low, which is approximately equal to the previous lows. This forms the second trough of the pattern.
The crucial element of the triple bottom pattern is the breakout above the resistance level, which connects the highs between the first and second lows. When the price decisively breaches this resistance, it generates a bullish signal, suggesting a potential trend reversal and a shift towards an uptrend.
To estimate price targets, traders may measure the vertical distance between the resistance level and the lowest point of the pattern (the second trough) and add it to the breakout point. As with any technical pattern, traders should exercise caution, employ additional technical indicators and risk management strategies, and look for confirmation signals when trading the triple bottom pattern, as false signals can occur. This pattern can be observed on various timeframes and is valuable for traders and investors across different financial markets.
